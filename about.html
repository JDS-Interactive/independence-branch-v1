What This Is / What This Isn’t

Independence Branch V1 is intentionally limited in scope. It’s designed to be transparent, non-partisan, and private by default.

What this is

  • A civic self-reflection tool — a short questionnaire to help individuals understand their civic values.
  • Deterministic and transparent — the same answers always produce the same results.
  • Private by default — results are generated locally in your browser.
  • Voluntary sharing — you decide whether to export or share anything.

What this is not

  • Not a vote or referendum — it does not create binding political outcomes.
  • Not a party test — it does not tell you who to support or how to vote.
  • Not public opinion — results are not representative of the nation.
  • Not identity verification — it does not verify citizenship, taxpayer status, or intent.

Privacy by Design

V1 is built so that your questionnaire responses and results are processed on your device. The site does not require an account to take the questionnaire, and V1 is designed to avoid collecting or storing individual responses on a server.

If you choose to export an image key, it is intended for your personal record and optional consistency verification. Social platforms may strip image metadata, so private result images are best treated as personal “vault” files.

Consistency Verification (What It Means)

If you export a private result image and later upload it to the verification page, the tool can check whether the embedded data matches the official V1 scoring logic.

  • Verification confirms consistency: the result matches the official logic and appears unaltered.
  • Verification does not confirm identity: it does not prove who shared the result.

Transparency & Restraint

V1 is intentionally modest in the claims it makes. It aims to be clear, auditable, and honest about its limits. Future versions may explore additional features only if they preserve integrity and trust.

Guiding Principle
Clarity before authority. Transparency before influence. Integrity before scale.
